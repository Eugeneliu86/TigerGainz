Exercise Guide Page

Oftentimes, creating, starting, or sticking with workouts can feel quite daunting and uncertain. As a result, it may be difficult to motivate yourself and your buddy or group, especially with all the Princeton work.

BUT have no fear! Exercise is not only healthy for the body and mind, but it can also be fun to create and do —especially if you have TigerGainz workout buddies and groups!

To help you and your workout buddy or group, here are several sample workouts and guides for creating your own. These are only meant to be starting points and we hope you find the best variation that works for you.

Please feel free to explore on the Internet for more or ask your friends, peers, or even adults, especially those in the Princeton community (e.g. Campus Recreation’s Experts).

As always, make sure to stay safe on- and off-campus! Remember to stretch!

Happy Exercising!

    • Creating an Exercise Set

    To build an effective workout routine that you and your workout buddies can follow and enjoy, follow these guidelines:

    Stretch:
    To avoid injuries and reduce soreness, stretch before and after your workout.

    Divide your days:
    Spread out your intensity and make sure you get enough rest.

    Challenge yourself but don’t overdo it:
    To not tire yourself at the beginning, start off at a moderate level and then build into it.

    Vary it up:
    Make sure your workout has a variety of exercises to keep yourself engaged.
    Alternate exercises to use different muscles and not tire yourself as quickly.

    Repetition is key:
    doing shorter, repeated cycles is often more effective (and maybe a little more challenging) than doing one long set.

    Allow for recovery:
    Make sure you set aside time to rest between exercises, BUT don’t overextend your rest (a few seconds to half a minute is usually adequate).

    Adapt your workout:
    Your workout preferences will change, so adjust your workout accordingly.

    MOST IMPORTANTLY: FOLLOW YOUR WORKOUT, KEEP YOURSELF AND YOUR BUDDY OR GROUP ACCOUNTABLE. YOU CAN DO IT!

    Sample Exercise Set:

    STRETCHES + Abdominals + Upper Body + Lower Body

    Total Length: 30 Minutes (42 Minutes for Three Rounds)
    Equipment: None
    On- Or Off-Campus: Both

    1. STRETCHING:

    Standing Hamstring Stretch (30 seconds)
    Tricep Stretch (15 seconds each arm)
    “Cat” Stretch (30 seconds)
    “Snake”/”Sphinx” Stretch (30 seconds)
    Standing Quad Stretch (30 seconds)

    2. ABDOMINALS, UPPER-BODY, LOWER-BODY, CYLCLE:
    Repeat 2 Times (3 Times if you want a CHALLENGE)

    Front Planks (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Squats (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Push-Up/Plank Taps (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    6-inch Leg Lifts (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Wall-Sit (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Push-Ups (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Sit-Ups (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Reverse Lunges (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Burpee with Push-Ups (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Crunches (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Forward Lunges (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)
    Tricep Dips (40 seconds, 20 seconds rest)

    1 Minute Break (DRINK WATER)

    3. STRETCHING:

    Standing Hamstring Stretch (30 seconds)
    Tricep Stretch (15 seconds each arm)
    “Cat” Stretch (30 seconds)
    “Snake”/”Sphinx” Stretch (30 seconds)
    Standing Quad Stretch (30 seconds)
